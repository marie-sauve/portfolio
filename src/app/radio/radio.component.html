Radio

I started my career as a journalist working for the radio, a good old-fashioned medium that would become my favourite way of transmitting and consuming news and entertainment. For four years I worked for the BBC, producing news and current affairs programmes in Northern Ireland. There, I wrote broadcast scripts ready for transmission, researched stories and interviewed talents, and reported live on air. I also produced and edited audio packages for programmes of all genres.

But radio didn't simply work for me. It became first and foremost a passion. In my free time I joined a community station where I initially produced and hosted a weekly three-hour music show. Later on, I curated, produced, and presented a 90mn-long specialist programme. This weekly meeting behind the mic quickly became my favourite thing in the world.
