About Me

Looking for an experienced storyteller who takes to any format like duck to water? Look no further. I’ve been working as a multimedia journalist for the past eight years, both for international newsrooms such as Storyful and the BBC, and on indie projects like documentaries, podcasts, and features.

There’s nothing I like more than a good story and a witty format, and let us be clear: one cannot come without the other. We’ve all watched or listened to a story where the content was fundamentally good but the form was soporific, and I’ve made it my life mission to go against this dreadful flow.

I attend the concert hall, the opera, theatres and the cinema more often than one should ever admit, and I live, sleep, and eat storytelling. Before I sold my soul to news verification, I was a film buff through and through. There’s nothing I like more than storyboarding, shooting, and editing. I have been experimenting with every possible format: live music reviews, radio programmes, podcasts, written features, documentary, portraits, film reviews – the list goes on.

I am a Jack of all trades of sorts, and I love to create multimedia packages when I work on a story as it makes my brain bubble. Mostly, my curiosity is bottomless and so I like to think I can bring fresh ideas and a fresh eye to any project.
